    <h1>Xinyue Wang</h1>
    <p class="role">University of Cologne · CGS in Management, Economics &amp; Social Sciences</p>

    <p>
      I am a doctoral student at the
      <a href="https://cgs.uni-koeln.de/en/">Cologne Graduate School in Management, Economics and Social Sciences</a>,
      University of Cologne. My research lies in
      <strong>applied microeconomics</strong>, with particular interest in
      <em>behavioral, personnel, and health economics</em>.
    </p>

    <p>
      I study how individuals and groups make decisions under real-world frictions —
      how preferences, incentives, and information environments shape behavior,
      and what this means for the design of policies that affect welfare.
    </p>

    <p>
      Previously, I obtained an M.Sc. in Economics from the University of Bonn under the supervision of
      <a href="https://www.econ.uni-bonn.de/iame/en/team/dohmen">Prof. Dr. Thomas Dohmen</a>,
      and worked as a research assistant at the
      <a href="https://www.econ.uni-bonn.de/iame/en">Institute for Applied Microeconomics</a>.
      I completed my undergraduate studies in China and worked with
      <a href="https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Jiajia-Li-3">Prof. Dr. Jiajia Li</a>'s team
      on projects in energy economics.
    </p>
